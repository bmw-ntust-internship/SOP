## 1. Parties

This Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between:

**Disclosing Party:** Prof. Ray-Guang Cheng, Supervisor of the Broadband Multimedia Wireless Networks (BMW) Laboratory, Department of Electronic and Computer Engineering, National Taiwan University of Science and Technology ("the Lab"); and

**Receiving Party:** the undersigned student or researcher ("the Member").

---

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" refers to all non-public information pertaining to the Lab, including but not limited to:

1. Unpublished research results, data, models, algorithms, and methodologies
2. Source code, software tools, and system configurations developed within the Lab
3. Internal Lab policies, procedures, and financial arrangements (e.g., stipend terms, compensation agreements, or any special arrangements made between the Member and the Professor)
4. Internal communications, decisions, and discussions between Lab members, the Professor, or university administration
5. Personnel information, student performance records, and internal evaluations
6. Any information shared within the Lab environment that has not been explicitly authorized for external distribution

---

## 3. Obligations of the Member

The Member agrees to the following obligations, which take effect immediately upon signing and continue after departure from the Lab:

1. **Maintain confidentiality.** The Member shall not disclose, distribute, publish, or otherwise share any Confidential Information with any person or entity outside the Lab without the Professor's prior written consent.

2. **Prevent misrepresentation.** The Member shall not make statements, claims, or representations about the Lab's internal policies, procedures, or agreements that are inaccurate, misleading, or taken out of context.

3. **Refrain from distributing rumors.** The Member shall not spread unverified information, rumors, or accounts of internal Lab discussions to other students, researchers, or any external parties.

4. **Protect research integrity.** The Member shall not use any unpublished research, data, or methodologies developed within the Lab for personal academic or professional gain after departure, without explicit written authorization from the Professor.

5. **Return Lab materials.** Upon departure, the Member shall return all Lab property — including equipment, access credentials, and proprietary materials — and delete or destroy any copies of Confidential Information in the Member's possession, unless otherwise authorized.

---

## 4. Exceptions

The obligations in Section 3 do not apply to information that:

1. Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Member;
2. Was independently known to the Member prior to joining the Lab and can be substantiated by written records; or
3. Is required to be disclosed by applicable law, court order, or government regulation — in which case the Member must promptly notify the Professor prior to any such disclosure.

---

## 5. Duration

This Agreement takes effect upon signing and remains in force as follows:

- **Indefinitely** — for unpublished research results, source code, datasets, and methodologies, until such information is publicly released by the Lab or the Professor grants written authorization; and
- **For three (3) years** from the Member's official departure date — for all other Confidential Information covered under this Agreement.

---

## 6. Acknowledgement of Lab Policies

The Member acknowledges that:

1. All personal arrangements, agreements, or exceptions made between the Member and the Professor are specific to the Member's individual circumstances and are **not** representative of the Lab's general policies or standard practices.
2. The Member shall not communicate, imply, or represent to other students, prospective members, or any external parties the specific terms of any personal arrangement — including but not limited to compensation, workload exceptions, or departure conditions — that was made between the Member and the Professor.

---

## 7. Consequences of Breach

A breach of this Agreement may result in:

- Academic and disciplinary action in accordance with NTUST regulations;
- Civil liability under the laws of the Republic of China (Taiwan).

---

## 8. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Republic of China (Taiwan). Any disputes arising from this Agreement shall be resolved in the courts of Taiwan.
